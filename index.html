2. ORIGIN & FORMATION: FROM QUANTUM FOAM TO KING OF PLANETS

Cosmic Origins: The First-Born Planet – formed just 1 million years after Sun. 13.8 bn years ago: Big Bang creates quarks → protons/neutrons → H (75%), He (25%). Multiple stellar generations fuse heavier elements, supernovae scatter them. 4.6 bn years ago: molecular cloud collapses, forming Sun and protoplanetary disk.

The Frost Line: Jupiter formed just beyond the frost line at ~5 AU, where water condensed. This location was crucial: inside frost line only rocky materials; at frost line water vapor condensed creating a pressure trap; beyond abundant ice and rock.

Formation Timeline – Core Accretion Model:

StageTime after SS formationEvent
Stage 10-1 million yearsDust condensation, planetesimal formation
Stage 21-3 million yearsIcy planetesimals coalesce beyond frost line
Stage 33-5 million yearsPlanetary embryo reaches 10-18 Earth masses (M⊕)
Stage 45-6 million yearsRapid gas accretion begins – embryo reaches 150 M⊕ in <1,000 years
Stage 56-10 million yearsFinal mass of 318 M⊕ achieved, creates gap in protoplanetary disk
Stage 610-100 million yearsMoon system forms, migration occurs

Rapid gas accretion: once embryo reached ~20 M⊕, it doubled mass every few hundred years.

The Grand Tack Hypothesis: Formation distance ~3.5 AU, inward migration to ~1.5 AU, then outward to final 5.2 AU (Saturn resonance). Timescale 3-6 million years. Scattered material, limited Mars’s growth, delivered water to Earth.

Jupiter's Primordial Size (2025 research): Young Jupiter was twice current diameter – could fit 8,000 Earths (now 1,300). Magnetic field then 50× stronger. Planet contracted as it cooled.

5. ENERGY & HEAT

Jupiter radiates 1.67× more energy than it receives from the Sun. Internal power: 3.35 × 10¹⁷ W. Heat flux at cloud tops: 5.4 W/m². Effective temperature: 124 K (110 K without internal heat).

Sources: Gravitational contraction (Kelvin-Helmholtz) ~90%, helium rain ~10%, primordial heat minor. Planet shrinks ~1 cm/year.

9. ATMOSPHERIC FEATURES & WEATHER

Banded Structure: Zones (bright, high, cool, rising) and Belts (dark, low, warm, sinking). Jet streams up to 600 km/h. Stratospheric jets near poles exceed 900 mph (ALMA).

The Great Red Spot (GRS): Size ~16,000 × 12,000 km (shrinking). Historical size in 1800s: 48,000 km. Rotation period ~6 days, winds 400 km/h, top temp ~110 K. Possibly same storm observed since 1665. Heats upper atmosphere.

Other Storms: White ovals, brown barges, polar cyclones (clusters at both poles). Lightning comparable to Earth's, superbolts. Auroras most powerful in Solar System (Io footprint, main oval).

14. MAGNETIC FIELD & MAGNETOSPHERE

Field Properties: Magnetic moment 1.55×10²⁷ Am² (20,000× Earth). Surface field 4.3 gauss (equator), polar ~14 gauss. Dipole tilt ~10°. Great Blue Spot anomaly near equator.

Magnetosphere: Largest coherent structure – dayside magnetopause 65-100 R♃, magnetotail extends beyond Saturn (600 million km). Radiation belts extremely hazardous. Plasma sheet from Io.

17. LIFE POTENTIAL

On Jupiter itself: Extremely unlikely – no liquid water, extreme pressures/temperatures. Cloud decks at 0.5-5 bar have T/p that could theoretically support extremophiles, but no liquid water.

On Europa and Ganymede: Promising – subsurface oceans, tidal heating. JUICE (arriving 2031) and Europa Clipper (2030) will investigate.

18. LIGHT & EMISSIONS

Reflected light: Albedo 0.51, magnitude -2.7. Color yellowish-white with reddish bands. Thermal emission: Peak ~10-20 μm, brightness temp ~124 K. Radio: Decametric (Io-controlled), decimetric (synchrotron), auroral kilometric radiation. UV/X-ray auroras: Brightest in Solar System.

20. BLACK HOLE POSSIBILITY

Current state: NOT a black hole candidate – mass too small by factor millions, density 1.33 g/cm³. Schwarzschild radius would be ~2.8 m. If Jupiter became black hole, same gravity, moons would orbit unchanged, but darkness.

21. WHY JUPITER IS NOT A STAR ("FAILED STAR" QUESTION)

ObjectMass (Earth masses)
Jupiter318 M⊕
Minimum brown dwarf4,000 M⊕ (13 M♃)
Minimum red dwarf24,000 M⊕ (80 M♃)

Jupiter would need 13× current mass for deuterium fusion, 80× for hydrogen fusion. It stopped growing because it ran out of gas and cleared its orbit.

22. ATOMIC & QUANTUM BEHAVIOR

States of matter: Atmosphere (molecular gas), deep atmosphere (supercritical fluid), molecular H₂ envelope (dense supercritical), metallic H (liquid metallic, degenerate electrons), core (high-pressure solid/fluid). Quantum effects: Electron degeneracy, pressure ionization, magnetic dynamo, synchrotron radiation.

Quarks → protons/neutrons → nuclei → atoms → molecules → planet (via Big Bang, stellar nucleosynthesis, solar nebula, accretion, runaway gas accretion).

23. LARGE-SCALE PROPERTIES

Mass: 1.898×10²⁷ kg = 1,898,000,000,000,000,000,000,000 tons. Surface area 62 billion km², volume 1.43 trillion km³. Atmosphere mass ~1×10²⁰ kg. Atoms in Jupiter ~10⁵⁴. Walking around equator would take 25 years. Jupiter's mass in humans: 3×10²².
